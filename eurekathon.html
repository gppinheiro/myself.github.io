Introduction

Nowadays, we live in a complicated worldwide situation. The Covid-19 pandemic is affecting our families, communities and businesses. With each passing day, poverty increases. Leading to the inevitable result of more people needing help to avoid starvation for their families.

The “Banco Alimentar Contra a Fome” is fundamental requirement to thousands of people, especially when demand for obtaining food is at an all-time high. This organization operated mainly dependent on the donations is receives, with this in mind we focus on the creation of the following question.

"How can we expand the “Banco Alimentar Contra a Fome” ’s donor base?"

Our solution involved these 3 topics:

1. Possibility to assign each NOS (Portugal Telecommunication operator) client a probability of making donations;

2. Identification of a potential donor’s profile;

3. Categorization of parishes where there is a bigger discrepancy between the potential donation of residents and donations that were effectively made to the “Banco Alimentar”.


Development

Firstly, we built a model that allows us to distinguish between people with a low donation potential and those with a high potential.

This classification is based on information from NOS customer base (general things like residence parish, their mobile contract and their television services).

This information is then cross-referenced with social and demographic metrics, such as the population, unemployment rate or percentage of beneficiaries of Social Income Insertion (in Portugal, we call RSI) in the residence municipality of the client.

Based on an indicator if the client has done any altruistic action during a given month (for example, if he made a donation), we created a predictive model. This model assigns each customer a probability if they will donate next month.

Our model allows us to choose potential donors 8 times more efficiently than if we did it randomly. For example, if we want to send a message to 10,000 customers to encourage them to donate, the model will hit 8x more at potential donors than a random model.

Secondly, with this predictive model, we also created a potential donor’s profile, through an inspection of the following variables.

The model detects a pattern with the following information from our NOS client:

- Lives mainly in urban areas (like Porto or Lisbon);

- Subscribes premium TV channels;

- Has three types SIM cards (anonymized)

- Doesn’t have debts to NOS

Last but not least, we created a prescriptive model that allows us to identify where there is a greater discrepancy between donations to the “Banco Alimentar Contra a Fome”, and the number of people in the parish with high potential to make donations.

Firstly, we added the probabilities of a person donating to the residence parish. We described these values as the altruism index.

Secondly, we used food donated per day data from a campaign in a supermarket, which is located in Lisbon. Then, we added this information to the parish, normalizing by the number of residents. This allowed us to obtain information about the amount donated per person in the parish, which we called donation index. Finally, we compared the donation index with altruism index. With that, we identified those parishes where there is bigger potential to increase the amount food donated.

For example, this value is particularly high in residential and family parishes, like Benfica and São Domingos de Benfica.


Conclusion

Our solution allows not only to increase the donor base, but also to raise more per donor. With this information that allows to better know its current and potential donors better, “Banco Alimentar” will be able to develop more targeted and effective marketing strategies for people who already donate and those who do not yet donate, but may donate.

In this sense, we recommended 3 possible applications of our model:

1. Knowing NOS customers who are more likely to make donations, one possibility would be to send SMS and/ or emails to them about when campaigns will take place and where they should go to donate.

2. Introduction of notes in NOS invoices.

3. Introduction of campaigns aimed at certain parishes. That is, for example, organize stronger campaigns, with the support of more supermarkets, with the display of posters and the distribution of pamphlets, especially in the parishes that our model identified as having the greatest discrepancy between donations to the “Banco Alimentar” and with a higher probability of people donating. This way, we can also understand if there is any logistical problem within the parish. For example, if we have more or less participating supermarkets.

Similar solutions have already been used in other contexts. In the 2012 election, Barack Obama primarily used public and marketing company data to create an index of how persuasive a potential voter would be. This has enabled them to reach citizens who are more likely to vote for it with more targeted advertising - for example, with emails and posters in the neighborhoods.

With our solution, “Banco Alimentar Contra a Fome” will be able to improve the capacity to obtain the necessary resources to fight hunger in Portugal. As this is directly dependent on the number of donations from individuals and companies, it is imperative to be able to increase the amount of food given, in order to be able to continue to respond to the need. Which will only improve the lives of those that utilize a service like this, creating a better future.

Prize

We received 2000€, from which 20% was donated to Nuvem Vitória .

Nuvem Vitória is an association whose volunteers tell stories for children who are away from their family environments every night.

By reading/hearing a story, we open our imagination and live a dream at that moment. For these children, being able to end the day in a dream ends up giving them more strength to recover more quickly. A relaxed environment is created and the stress level decreases.

Because Nuvem Vitória is an innovative and recent project, donations are smaller. So we, Hunger Byte, gave ours to this non-governmental organization. In this way, we know that it is an amount that will help them a lot.
